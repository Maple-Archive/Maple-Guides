MapleStory Complete First Adversary Guide

By iSIingGunz

This guide will be covering specifics for every difficulty in this fight. There will be attacks and mechanics in this fight that hit or act differently depending on the difficulty, so they will be labeled in a “Easy/Normal/Hard/Extreme” format.

Standard Information

Entry Level Requirement:

Level 270 for all difficulties

Boss Level:

Easy: Level 270 (380% PDR)

Normal: Level 280 (380% PDR)

Hard: Level 285 (380% PDR)

Extreme: Level 290 (380% PDR)

Maximum Party Members:

3 for Easy/Normal/Hard/Extreme

Sacred Force Requirement: 220/320/340/460

This is a boss that has a difficulty designated for every single stage in the game. You are barely strong enough to try out easy mode in a full party once you get yourself through all of the basic events (Hyper Burning, Burning Beyond, etc) and a little bit of gearing, as party spec for easy mode is very similar to soloing ctenes. The solo spec for this boss is between soloing Easy Kalos and Hard Seren.

You can then start running Normal mode once you are strong enough to solo easy mode. Through normal mode, you can finally start your Eternal Equipment progression from this boss, as it provides Hat, Top, Bottom, & Shoulder. In order to solo Normal mode, you’ll want your character to be strong enough to complete the Normal Kalos Legion Champion mission.

There is a significant jump in difficulty between Normal and Hard Mode. Not only does the boss gain nearly 6x more HP, but it starts to become more punishing as well. You’ll likely want to solo Normal Adversary in about 15 minutes before considering running Hard Mode in a full party. The specs for this boss are roughly around Normal Baldrix level.

Extreme mode is for the top of the top players. You’ll want to be slightly above the Hard Mode solo spec unless you’re running with very strong party classes (Lynn). Alongside Extreme Kaling, this boss has the highest party spec requirement in the game.

This boss can be bound. However, critical bind is disabled in this boss (Zero and maybe Mo Xuan). This was purposefully done to avoid any interruptions with parrying attacks.

Entry Limit: None

Clear Limit: 1 time per week. The clear verdict is when you have defeated Phase 3. Easy, Normal, Hard, & Extreme have shared clear limits.

Time Limit: 30 Minutes

Life Count: 1000 Enemy’s Will (More on this soon)

Potion Cooldown: 10 seconds

Enemy’s Will

This is an individual life count that is set at 1000 when the fight begins. If a player were to reach 0, they would die out of the fight. If you die during the fight, 200 Enemy’s Will will be deducted. You can also lose even more depending on where your gauge stands during the Cycle of Power.

(cycle of power for each phase in the fight)

Every 2 minutes and 18 seconds, the Cycle of Power will occur. There will be a warning 5 seconds before it happens. When this occurs, the entire party’s Enemy’s Will is reduced based on the remaining Power of Order (gauge system). If the Power of Order is below Level 3 (40% filled), then the gauge will return to 50% filled after the Cycle of Power.

It is also important to note that after the Cycle of Power, the boss can be attacked as he is respawning.

Immortal Will

This skill right here is the key to fighting this boss. It is just simply parrying. You need to press your NPC chat key in order to use this skill. After pressing this skill, it makes you completely invincible from Adversary’s attacks for 0.5 seconds. However, this NEEDS to be used for specific attacks as I talk about them throughout this guide.

After you successfully parry those attacks, you will hear a unique success sound, increase your Power of Order, and reduce the cooldown of the parry.

The skill has a 6 second cooldown by default, but successfully parrying certain attacks will decrease this cooldown by 4.5 seconds. You can still successfully parry attacks even when you are iframed.

This skill can be used during every single type of attack (keydowns included) except for Origin and Ascent.

Gauge System

Also known as the Power of Order. When beginning each phase, the gauge starts at 500 and decreases by 5 every second (can go up to 1000). The gauge will decrease significantly if you get hit by an attack that you are meant to parry.

In Easy and Normal mode, you only have to dodge his powerful attacks. However, in Hard and Extreme mode, every single one of his attacks, map patterns included, will decrease your gauge.

In this guide, the gauge fluctuations described with each pattern will be based on a solo run. So if you’re running as a duo or trio, here is what you need to keep in mind:

Duo: The gauge increase is 50% of that of a solo run, and the gauge decrease is 75%/75%/75%/100% of that of a solo run.

Trio: The gauge increase is 34% of that of a solo run, and the gauge decrease is 50%/50%/50%/100% of that of a solo run.

Your final damage will be increased or decreased depending on the amount of gauge you have. And the amount of Enemy’s Will reduction during the Cycle of Power will depend as well:

The final damage change is the reason why this may be the most skill based boss in the game. The damage difference between Stage 1 and Stage 5 is literally double. For parties who are on a strict minimum cut spec for this boss, they are nearly required to maintain Stage 5.

1 update after Adversary was released on KMS, it was actually changed to where the gauge would maintain Stage 4 or above after the Cycle of Power if it was already at that stage to begin with. Beforehand, no matter how high you got the gauge, it would always reset to Stage 3 after the Cycle of Power. We are currently unsure if other regions will maintain the gauge on the boss release.

Phase 1

This is the Courage of the Adversary. He embodies the Warrior and Thief jobs, using a Two Handed Sword and a Dagger. It is important to note that in Phase 1, some attacks will be paired with Shadow Partners, and that these Shadow Partners will attack you first before Adversary does himself.

Phase 1: Map Patterns

Spatial Slash:

10 sets of lasers are created at 0.3 second intervals. More lasers will be created if your gauge is at a higher stage (+2 per stage, up to 18 at stage 5). The lasers will attack 1.2 seconds after they spawn. This attack deals 10% damage and decreases your Power of Order gauge by 0/0/5/10.

Cooldown: 5 seconds (10 seconds before first use)

Whirlwind:

This pattern is added whenever your Power of Order gauge reaches Stage 5. 2 seconds after reaching Stage 5, a whirlwind is created on the left and right sides of the map and it will last until your gauge falls below Stage 4. Staying in the whirlwind deals 25% damage every second while slowing you for 1 second, ignoring status resistance. It will also decrease your Power of Order gauge by 0/0/10/20.

Warrior’s Mark:

This is an instanced pattern. A mark will appear at your location and then stop after 3 seconds. After it stops, it begins to attack after 0.6 seconds and attacks will constantly occur every 0.48 seconds. This attack deals 15% damage per tick and applies the Potion Lock debuff for 3 seconds, ignoring status resistance. It will also decrease your Power of Order gauge by 0/0/5/10.

Cooldown: 25 seconds (10 seconds before first use)

Phase 1: Adversary Patterns

(Binding the boss will cancel out any of these patterns)

Teleport:

Adversary will teleport to a random player’s location and will try to attack them. The teleport only activates if the player is a certain distance away from him. This attack deals 30%/30%/30%/80% damage and applies a 3 second stun. On Hard/Extreme, status resistance is ignored. It will also decrease your Power of Order gauge by 0/0/30/50.

Cooldown: 5 seconds

Double Swing:

Adversary will swing his greatsword at you twice.

The 1st attack will be swung in a narrow range in front of you. This attack deals 30% damage and decreases your Power of Order gauge by 0/0/30/50. You can avoid this attack by ducking.

The 2nd attack is a strengthened attack that you can parry to increase the gauge. Adversary will swing the sword down at you dealing 50%/50%/50%/80% damage, inflict a super knockback, and decrease the Power of Order gauge by 200/200/200/300. However, a successful parry will increase the gauge by 100/75/75/75. You can also avoid this attack by ducking.

It is important to keep note that when doing the 2nd attack, Adversary can change directions immediately before slamming the sword down depending on if the player he is aggroing has moved behind him. Party members should ideally stay on the same side or be prepared to crouch to avoid being attacked.

Cooldown: ~10 seconds

Triple Swing:

Adversary will swing his greatsword at you thrice.

The 1st and 2nd attacks are done around the same time, with each attack dealing 20% damage and decreases the Power of Order gauge by 0/0/15/25. You can avoid these attacks by ducking.

The 3rd attack is a strengthened attack that will be charged up for 1.8 seconds, and Adversary will immediately switch directions before winding up the 3rd attack if he has to, depending on if the aggro has moved. This attack deals 50%/50%/50%/80% damage, inflict a super knockback, and decreases the Power of Order gauge by 200/200/200/300. In Hard/Extreme, you are stunned for 0.8 seconds, ignoring status resistance. A successful parry will increase the gauge by 100/75/75/75.

Cooldown: 10 seconds

Greatsword Shower:

Adversary will summon numerous swords into the sky, and then slam them onto the ground for them to explode.

For the 1st attack, the spawned swords will hit the ground after 3 seconds. This attack can be avoided by up jumping. This attack deals 90% damage and decreases your Power of Order gauge by 0/0/60/100.

For the 2nd attack, the swords will explode after 2 seconds. This is a strengthened attack that can be avoided by up jumping even higher compared to the 1st attack. This attack deals 1000% damage and decreases the Power of Order gauge by 200/200/400/500. However, a successful parry will increase the gauge by 200.

Cooldown: 55 seconds

If the boss is not bound on entry, this will be the very first pattern that he casts. Some parties will wait to parry this attack to get the gauge to Stage 4 for +10% final damage before bursting.

Shadow Charge (this attack may be paired with a Shadow Partner):

Adversary will charge forward, slashing you with his daggers. The attacks can be avoided by up jumping.

When the wind up begins, he will charge after 1.38 seconds. This is a strengthened attack. This attack deals 50%/50%/50%/80% damage, inflicts super knockback, and stun you for 0.5 seconds while ignoring status resist. It will also decrease the Power of Order gauge by 200/200/200/300. However, a successful parry will increase the gauge by 100/75/75/75.

If the attack is paired with a Shadow Partner, the Shadow Partner will charge forward first after 1.32 seconds. ONLY the Shadow Partner’s charge can be avoided by ducking. This attack deals 20% damage, inflicts super knockback, and stuns you for 5 seconds. It will also decrease the Power of Order gauge by 0/0/30/50. After the Shadow Partner attacks, Adversary will then attack after 1.14 seconds.

Cooldown: 12 seconds

Shadow Sweep (Part 1 and 3 of this attack may be paired with a Shadow Partner):

This is a 3 part attack, with parts 1 and 3 being strengthened attacks. Once again, if a Shadow Partner spawns, they will attack first before the main body.

Part 1: He will charge after 1.5 seconds. This attack deals 50%/50%/50%/80% damage and inflicts super knockback. It will also decrease your Power of Order gauge by 200/200/200/300. However, a successful parry will increase the gauge by 100/75/75/75.

If a Shadow Partner spawns, it will charge forward after 2 seconds. ONLY the Shadow Partner’s charge can be avoided by ducking. This attack deals 20% damage, inflicts super knockback, and will stun you for 5 seconds. It will also decrease your Power of Order gauge by 0/0/30/50. After the Shadow Partner attacks, Adversary will then attack after 1.35 seconds.

Part 2: He will charge after 1 second of setting up his stance for this part. This attack deals 30% damage, inflicts super knockback, and decreases your Power of Order gauge by 0/0/30/50. This attack can be avoided by ducking.

Part 3: He will charge after 2 seconds of setting up his stance for this part. This attack deals 50%/50%/50%/80% damage and inflicts super knockback. It will also decrease your Power of Order gauge by 200/200/200/300. However, a successful parry will increase the gauge by 100/75/75/75.

If a Shadow Partner spawns, it will charge forward after 2.28 seconds. ONLY the Shadow Partner’s charge can be avoided by a regular jump if timed properly (or just up jump). This attack deals 20% damage, inflicts super knockback, and will stun you for 5 seconds. It will also decrease your Power of Order gauge by 0/0/30/50. After the Shadow Partner attacks, Adversary will then attack after 1.32 seconds.

Cooldown: 12 seconds

Intense Strike:

Adversary will disappear, and then shortly reappear on the map alongside 2 clones. After 2.28 seconds, the entire map will be attacked. Adversary will be invincible during this entire sequence. The objective is to find the real Adversary, who will have a red glow on his daggers that will fade after 1 second, indicating that it is a strengthened attack.

Being attacked by a clone will deal 80% damage and decrease the Power of Order gauge by 0/0/60/100. Being attacked by the main body will deal 999% damage and decrease the Power of Order gauge by 200/200/400/500. However, successfully parrying the main body will increase the gauge by 200.

Cooldown: 55 seconds (30 seconds before first use)

If your parry skill is not up for this pattern, you must either iframe, tank a clone attack with full HP, or duck in the far left or right corner of the map.

Before the Cycle of Power in this phase occurs, the following patterns can no longer be used:

15 seconds: Whirlwind

10 seconds: Greatsword Shower, Shadow Sweep, Intense Strike

5 seconds: All Patterns

That is all for Phase 1. In the video guide, I will go over some distinct parrying tips for particular attacks.

Phase 2

This is the Faith of the Adversary. He will now wield the weapons of the Magician and the Archer. The Magician has Fire, Ice, and Holy attributes, but attacks based on these attributes do not have any unique effects.

Upon entering this phase, unique attributes will be randomly assigned above the character. Whichever attribute you have above your head will be the attacks that you need to parry to increase the Power of Order gauge. Attributes will be randomly reassigned every Cycle of Power.

Phase 2: Map Patterns

Infinity:

“Infinity-like” (explorer magician skill) previews are made throughout the map and will attack that location after 2.46 seconds. This is an attribute attack, so you must find the infinity with the same attribute as yours and parry it. This attack deals 30%/30%/30%/50% damage and decreases the Power of Order gauge by 50/50/100/150. However, a successful parry will increase the gauge by 100. There are 10 possible locations that infinity can be used in, but there will only be 6 (2 of each attribute) every time it’s used.

Cooldown: 6 seconds

Raining Debris:

A vertical pillar-like preview appears on the map, and shortly after debris will fall on that location. This attack deals 30%/30%/30%/50% damage, stuns you for 1 second, and decreases the Power of Order gauge by 0/0/50/150.

Cooldown: 6 seconds.

The debris can spawn on top of portals, which makes it annoying when trying to deal with attacks like Element of Chaos & Elemental Torrent. If you are having trouble trying to deal with these attacks with the Debris without having an iframe, you should tank the debris and save Hero’s Will for it to avoid getting stunned.

Altar:

A random player is marked, and an Altar will spawn on top of them after 5.48 seconds. If the player is airborne when the Altar spawns, it will fall to the ground or the platforms depending on where the player was. After 1.26 seconds, it will start constant attacks every 0.5 seconds for 25 seconds. Each attack deals 15%/15%/15%/30% damage and decreases the Power of Order gauge by 0/0/50/150.

Cooldown: 30 seconds (15 seconds before first use)

This attack in Hard/Extreme mode can rapidly drain the gauge, if partying with other players, you can see who has an Altar about to spawn on top of them and tell them to go to the corner to avoid making contact with it while it’s active. You need to be very careful, because if you have the Altar and quickly move right before it gets placed down, you will see the Altar normally, but your party members will see it in a different location, so it’s best to move to a corner asap and wait until it spawns.

Phase 2: Adversary Patterns

(Binding the boss will cancel out any of these patterns)

Element of Chaos:

Three lasers of different attributes are generated at the 2, 6, and 10 o’ clock positions, centered around the Adversary. There will be some arrows you can look at (that are kind of hard to see) between the lasers to see if it will move clockwise or counterclockwise. Afterwards, the lasers will then move for 1 full cycle. This attack deals 35%/35%/35%/80% damage every 0.4 seconds and decreases your Power of Order gauge by 200/200/200/300. However, a successful parry will increase the gauge by 200.

Cooldown: 65 seconds (3 seconds before first use)

Elemental Torrent:

Lasers of random attributes are generated at regular intervals vertically across the map. The lasers will have a gap that matches the height locations of the 1st and 2nd floors. After some time, the lasers will move in 1 direction (there will be arrows pointing either left or right). You can dodge this entire sequence by standing on top of the portal (but be careful of debris), while facing and parrying lasers with your attribute. This attack deals 15%/15%/15%/80% damage and decreases your Power of Order gauge by 200/200/200/300. However, a successful parry increases the gauge by 150/100/100/100.

Cooldown: 65 seconds (30 seconds before first use)

Attribute Arrows:

This is an instanced pattern. Multiple crossbows are arranged in a circle around the Adversary, and he will then quickly fire arrows directly at you at the same time. A minimum of 3 arrows are created, but for each Stage the Power of Order gauge is on, the amount of arrows is increased by 1 (up to 7 arrows on Stage 5). This is an attribute attack of your set attribute. Each arrow deals 10%/10%/10%/20% damage and decreases your Power of Order gauge by 10/10/20/30 per arrow. However, if you parry at least 1 arrow, the Power of Order gauge increases by 125/100/100/100. This increase is only applied 1 time, no matter how many arrows you parry.

Cooldown: 20 seconds (5 seconds before first use)

Arrow Scatter:

Adversary will hold a bow, pulling the string. He will prepare to attack 1 of 4 different directions (top left, top right, bottom left, bottom right). After a while, an arrow is fired, and once it collides with a corner of the map, it splits into 9 arrows that travel across the map. You take 50% damage when hit by the main arrow, and 10% damage when hit by any of the smaller arrows. All arrows decrease the Power of Order gauge by 0/0/50/100.

Cooldown: 35 seconds (5 seconds before first use)

It is quite difficult to dodge these arrows without an iframe. While you don’t really have to do so in Easy/Normal, you’ll really want to in Hard/Extreme. What usually works for me is that whenever he is firing arrows, whether it's to the top or the bottom, I will stand by a portal on the opposite side he is facing, on the side of the portal that is closer to a corner, and crouch during the firing arrows. This MOSTLY works, but can have some interruptions with debris and what not.

Summon Evolve:

The Archer bird, Evolve, will be summoned by the Adversary. After 1.5 seconds, Evolve will fold or spread its wings to indicate where it is going to attack. It will then attack after 2.68 seconds. This attack deals 40%/40%/40%/80% damage, inflicts super knockback, and stun you for 1.5 seconds ignoring status resist. It will also decrease the Power of Order gauge by 0/0/100/200.

Here is how you can tell where Evolve is going to attack:

Both wings folded: Attacks the Center

Both wings spread wide: Attacks Left and Right

Only left wing folded: Attacks Left

Only right wing folded: Attacks Right

Cooldown: 20 seconds (5 seconds before first use)

Sniping:

This is an instanced pattern. A scope effect appears on top of your character, and at the same time, a crossbow will track your location. After a while, the crossbow becomes fixed, and then will rotate and fire 6 regular arrows at 0.3 second intervals. The crossbow’s rotation speed is limited and can only move by about 20 degrees between shots. So if you set the crossbow to shoot the opposite side of your character, then none of the arrows should reach you. Each arrow deals 20%/20%/20%/40% damage, inflicts a weak status, and decreases the Power of Order gauge by 0/0/50/100.

After the consecutive shots, the crossbow prepares its final attack. This is an attribute attack of your set attribute. The crossbow will continue to aim directly at your character’s location until the arrow fires. This attack deals 50%/50%/50%/999% damage and decreases the Power of Order gauge by 200/200/400/500. However, a successful parry increases the gauge by 250/200/200/200.

Cooldown: 45 seconds (5 seconds before first use)

One tip, for if you want to avoid this attack entirely, is to wait for the crossbow to lock in on your character's location, then jump to the center of Adversary and hold blink. None of the arrows, including the final one, will be able to hit you. However, this is an attack I would consider learning how to parry, due to the massive gauge increase if you manage to succeed.

Before the Cycle of Power in this phase occurs, the following patterns will no longer be used:

15 seconds: Element of Chaos

14 seconds: Elemental Torrent

13 seconds: Sniping

6 seconds: Summon Evolve

5 seconds: All Patterns

That is all for Phase 2. In the video guide, I will go over some distinct parrying tips for particular attacks.

Phase 3

The First Adversary embodies all 4 of the original professions. This phase reveals the Adversary’s true strength. This phase uses both attribute attacks and strengthened attacks. For strengthened attacks, his attacks will be in the color of a rainbow instead of a red effect to include the color of all attributes.

The Shadow Partners have returned. So once again, if an attack has a Shadow Partner paired with it, do note that these Shadow Partners will attack first before Adversary does.

Phase 3: Map Patterns

Spatial Spell:

This attack is similar to Spatial Slash in phase 1. However, the preview effect is only 1 second long. This attack deals 15% damage and decreases the Power of Order gauge by 0/0/5/15.

Phase 3: Adversary Patterns

(Binding the boss will cancel out any of these patterns)

Teleport:

Just like phase 1, Adversary will teleport to a random player’s location and will try to attack them. The teleport only activates if the player is a certain distance away from him. This attack deals 30%/30%/30%/80% damage and applies a 3 second stun. On Hard/Extreme, status resistance is ignored. It will also decrease your Power of Order gauge by 0/0/30/50.

Cooldown: 5 seconds

Triple Swing:

This attack is similar to phase 1’s Double Swing, but a new attack is added at the end.

1st Attack: Adversary will swing at you 0.75 seconds after preparing his stance. This attack deals 30% damage and decreases the Power of Order gauge by 0/0/30/50. You can avoid this attack by ducking.

2nd Attack: Adversary prepares his 2nd swing, and will attack after 1.5 seconds. This attack will have a random attribute applied to it. It deals 50%/50%/50%/80% damage, inflicts super knockback, and decreases the Power of Order gauge by 200/200/200/150. However, a successful parry will increase the gauge by 100/75/75/75. You can NOT avoid this attack by ducking. Just like Phase 1, he can change directions right before using this attack, so be prepared to dodge if you do not have the proper attribute.

3rd Attack: Adversary holds his sword vertically, preparing to swing it after 3.14 seconds. This is a strengthened attack. It deals 50%/50%/50%/200% damage, inflicts super knockback, and decreases the Power of Order gauge by 200/200/200/300. However, a successful parry will increase the gauge by 100/75/75/75. Adversary will change directions to the player he is aggroing if he has to right before charging this attack.

Cooldown: 10 seconds

Quadruple Swing:

This attack is similar to phase 1’s Triple Swing, but a new attack is added at the end.

1st & 2nd Attacks: Adversary will swing at you twice very shortly after preparing his stance. These attacks deal 20% damage and both attacks decrease the Power of Order gauge by 0/0/15/25. You can avoid both of these attacks by ducking.

3rd Attack: Adversary prepares his 3rd swing, and will attack after 1.86 seconds. This attack will have a random attribute applied to it. It deals 50%/50%/50%/80% damage, inflicts super knockback, and decreases the Power of Order gauge by 200/200/200/150. However, a successful parry will increase the gauge by 100/75/75/75. You can NOT avoid this attack by ducking. Adversary will change directions to the player he is aggroing if he has to right before charging this attack.

4th Attack: Adversary holds his sword at an angle, preparing to swing it after 2.37 seconds. This is a strengthened attack. It deals 50%/50%/50%/200% damage, inflicts super knockback, and decreases the Power of Order gauge by 200/200/200/300. However, a successful parry will increase the gauge by 100/75/75/75. Adversary will change directions to the player he is aggroing if he has to right before charging this attack.

Cooldown: 10 seconds

Shadow Sweep (All parts of this attack may be paired with a Shadow Partner):

The Shadow Sweep returns, but this time, it’s a fixed attribute attack. It is important to note that the 1st attack is Fire, the 2nd attack is Ice, and the 3rd attack is Holy. Prepare to dodge 2 of these while parrying the one with your attribute.

1st Attack: Adversary will get into stance and charge after 1.5 seconds. You can avoid this attack by ducking.

1st Attack (Shadow Partner version): The Shadow Partner attacks first after 1.89 seconds, then the main body attacks after 1.59 seconds. You can avoid all attacks by ducking.

2nd Attack: Adversary will get into stance once again and charge after 1.59 seconds. You can avoid this attack by ducking.

2nd Attack (Shadow Partner version): The Shadow Partner attacks first after 1.68 seconds, then the main body attacks after 0.78 seconds. You can avoid all attacks by ducking.

3rd Attack: Adversary will get into stance for the final time and charge after 2.28 seconds. You can avoid this attack with a regular jump at the correct time.

3rd Attack (Shadow Partner version): The Shadow Partner attacks first after 1.59 seconds, then the main body attacks after 0.87 seconds. You can avoid both attacks with a regular jump at the correct time.

All Shadow Partner attacks deal 20% damage, inflict super knockback, stun you for 5 seconds, and decrease your Power of Order gauge by 0/0/30/50.

All Main Body attacks deal 50%/50%/50%/80% damage, inflict super knockback, and decrease your Power of Order gauge by 200/200/200/300. However, a successful parry increases the gauge by 200/150/150/150.

Cooldown: 20 seconds

Elemental Flow:

Adversary draws a magic circle with his staff, and 3 magic circles of different attributes are created on the top of the map. The magic circles will be activated one by one at regular intervals. The location of each attribute and which one fires first are random with each cast.

When a circle is activated, a laser warning range is created based on your previous location, and then shortly after the laser will activate and track your character until the player with the proper attribute successfully parries it or enough time has passed. The activated laser is instanced. This attack ends when it goes through all 3 attributes. The lasers deal 20%/20%/20%/50% damage every 0.4 seconds and decrease your Power of Order gauge by 30/30/30/50 per tick. However, a successful parry increases the gauge by 250/200/200/200.

Cooldown: 55 seconds

If you do not need your Genesis iframe for burst, using it right before the 1st laser activates for Elemental Flow wouldn’t be a bad idea, as Adversary can continue to use other attacks (Except Arrow Rain and Spatial Spell) once he spawns the circles. If not Genesis, you could also use any other class iframe, or Ascent skills (if not needed in previous phases for pacing). It could just end up being too many things going on at once. You can also completely cancel this entire pattern with a bind.

Arrow Rain:

Adversary continuously fires arrows into the sky, launching a barrage of 45 arrows at random locations throughout the map. Each arrow deals 45%/45%/45%/60% damage and decreases your Power of Order gauge by 0/0/50/100.

Cooldown: 55 seconds (30 seconds before first use)

Once again, this is also a useful part of the fight to use Genesis iframe, Ascent skills (if not needed in previous phases for pacing), or any other iframe you may have.

Before the Cycle of Power in this phase occurs, the following patterns will no longer be used:

11 seconds: Shadow Sweep

10 seconds: Elemental Flow & Arrow Rain

5 seconds: All Patterns

That is all for Phase 3. In the video guide, I will go over some distinct parrying tips for particular attacks.

Death

You’ve cleared the boss! The First Adversary will kneel before you in defeat, and you can now enter the reward room.

Evaluation

This written guide was shorter than I expected (the Baldrix guide was on Page 82 by the time I finished Phase 3 for it…) but maybe that’s a good thing.

I think this is a really well made fight. Unlike Limbo and Baldrix, I believe the learning process for Adversary is on a reasonably difficult level (Speaking from experience specifically for Hard Mode). I think there might have been some negative reviews here and there on the difficulty spike going from Easy/Normal to Hard/Extreme, but I think it’s a good thing that the fight becomes much harder for the last 2 difficulties. I even think there is a MASSIVE gap in difficulty between Hard and Extreme mode. Extreme mode might be one of the most difficult prog bosses in the entire game.

They did a really good job on making the learning process hard, but reruns after your first clear are usually easy. This isn’t a boss like Kalos or Kaling where it takes multiple attempts to clear, but many reruns after your first clear have high fail potential due to whatever reason. However, I could be wrong about this regarding Extreme Adversary, as it seems very very easy to screw up min clears for that one.
