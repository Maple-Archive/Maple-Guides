MapleStory Complete Kai Guide

By iSIingGunz

This guide will be covering specifics for every difficulty in this fight. There will be attacks and mechanics in this fight that hit or act differently depending on the difficulty, so they will be labeled in a “Normal/Hard” format.

Standard Information

Entry Level Requirement:

Level 270 for Normal Mode

Level 280 for Hard Mode

Boss Level:

Normal: Level 270 (380% PDR)

Hard: Level 280 (380% PDR)

Maximum Party Members:

1 for Normal/Hard mode

The Time Wanderer Kai is a seasonal boss that is only available on and during the event period of Challengers World Season 3. Both Normal and Hard mode are fairly low spec bosses that help the growth of your character as you play in this new world. Because it is an event boss, its rewards are far superior to actual bosses that require around similar specs. You can only challenge this boss by yourself.

Normal Mode is comparable to soloing the Chaos Tenebris bosses (Chaos Gloom, Hard Darknell, Hard Verus Hilla). Hard mode is comparable to soloing Hard Seren.

Chrono Gauge

The Chrono Gauge continuously fills up as you fight the boss, but will decrease if you are hit by any of Kai’s patterns. When the Time Distortion occurs, you can use Chrono Break (Tab on image) by consuming the Chrono Gauge. The Chrono Gauge does not increase during the Time Distortion.

Chrono Break can ONLY be used during Time Distortion. When the timer on the left side reaches 0, Kai enters a time distorted state for 20 seconds, where the attacks he deals in this state are way too fast to dodge. You need to use Chrono Break to slow down time to evade attacks properly. Doing this will also make the Time Distortion state last longer than what’s stated. The 20 seconds will actually be more like ~33 seconds.

Chrono Break consumes 90 Chrono Gauge on entry, and will then consume 30 Chrono Gauge per second after 3 seconds. The Chrono Gauge is capped at 1000 and increases by 24 every second Chrono Break is not being used.

Chrono Step (Space on image) is an evasion skill that functions similar to the parrying in The First Adversary. Unlike First Adversary, this skill does not increase your final damage when used successfully. It is simply only meant to evade attacks.

Chrono Step will make your character move in the direction you are facing by default. It is recommended to hold the down arrow key while using this skill if you do not want to move.

Chrono Step holds up to 3 charges with a cooldown of 5 seconds per recharge. However, successfully evading an attack using Chrono Step while Chrono Break is active will instantly recharge Chrono Step 1 time. Even if you evade multiple attacks with 1 Chrono Step, the recharge limit is only limited to 1.

Time Distortion

Similarly to what was stated above, this is a state where Kai uses attacks that are so fast that you need to use Chrono Break to successfully evade attacks. Kai will not use any direct attack patterns 5 seconds before entering Time Distortion.

During Time Distortion, Kai is immune to being bound. If Kai is bound as he enters Time Distortion, he will immediately be released from the bind.

During Time Distortion, you are presented with a Fever Count in the middle of the screen. The Fever Count increases by approximately 600 per second (more like 360 per second while Chrono Break is active). The Fever Count can be decreased if you are hit by any patterns so it’s very important to be careful.

At the end of the Time Distortion, you will receive a fixed cooldown reduction on ALL of your skills as well as a special gimmick for reaching 10,000.

0~5,999: No effect

6,000~7,999: 10 second cooldown reduction on all skills

8,000~9,999: 20 second cooldown reduction on all skills

10,000: 40 second cooldown reduction on all skills AND Kai enters a groggy state for 10 seconds.

This means that if Kai is played properly, you will be bursting 1.5x more than usual. The boss has a 20 minute timer, but the amount of times you can burst will be equal to any boss with a 30 minute timer.

For classes that have their gauge filled up based on time, you will receive gauge equal to the time skip you are rewarded with by the end of the Time Distortion (so either 10/20/40 seconds).

Similar to Kalos, Kai is immune to being bound during the groggy state, outside of absolute (origin skill) bind.

There are a total of 6 Time Distortion Patterns. 3 unique for Phase 1 and 3 unique for Phase 2. During the Time Distortion, he will randomly use 2 of the 3 that are designated for the phase that you are fighting. These patterns will be explained more in detail later in the guide.

If Kai is to reach 50% HP during Time Distortion, it will be terminated immediately and your cooldowns will be refunded to you based on the Fever Count at the time of the phase change.

Common Patterns

These are patterns that will always appear during the fight in both phases.

The Rift of Time:

Green laser walls spawning on both ends of the map. These are here to discourage playing in the corners. However, the lasers will disappear during Time Distortion. This attack deals 30%/50% damage and decreases your Chrono Gauge by 10 on Hard Mode only.

Echo Fang:

3 Diagonal Lines generate on the map every 5 seconds. It divides the entire map into 8 spaces and attacks each space once. It does not activate during Time Explosions or Time Distortion. If Singularity Explosion occurs, it begins appearing after 5 seconds. This attack deals 10%/15% damage and decreases your Chrono Gauge by 5 on Hard Mode only.

Singularity Explosion:

Every 20 seconds, multiple green clocks spawn at the top of the map and then explode. This pattern does not appear during Time Distortion. This attack deals 15%/30% damage and decreases your Chrono Gauge by 15 on Hard Mode only.

Phase 1

When you enter the boss, the Phase 1 entry cutscene will begin to play within the fight. This will last for about 7 seconds before you can begin to hit him.

Swiftness:

Kai will teleport to you if you are a certain distance away from him, and he will strike you down with his sword from above, followed by another swing right after.

The first swing deals 20%/30% damage, stuns you for 1/4 seconds, and decreases your Chrono Gauge by 30/60.

The second swing deals 20%/30% damage, applies super knockback, and decreases your Chrono Gauge by 30/60.

Cooldown: 5 seconds

Double Swing:

Kai swings his sword upward once after 0.84 seconds, then it is followed by a downward strike after 1.44 seconds. Both attacks have identical range. If you are behind him as he is about to use his second swing, he will change to your direction.

The first swing deals 30%/40% damage and decreases your Chrono Gauge by 30/60.

The second swing deals 30%/40% damage, causes a slight knockback, stuns you for 1.5 seconds, and decreases your Chrono Gauge by 30/60.

Cooldown: 10 seconds

Recall Strike:

Kai displays an area of effect in front of him and then dashes in that direction. Afterwards, he will spawn a hologram that will attack either forward or backwards. Both attacks each deal 30%/40% damage, cause a knock back and a 1.5 second stun, and will also decrease your Chrono Gauge by 50/100.

Cooldown: 10 seconds

Energy Swing:

Kai swings his sword around himself, knocking back nearby targets while also firing energy waves to each side.

Kai’s swing deals 20%/30% damage, causes a knock back and a 1.5 second stun, and will also decrease your Chrono Gauge by 50/100.

The Sword Energy deals 50%/60% damage, causes a 5 second slow effect, and decreases your Chrono Gauge by 70/140.

If Kai manages to knock you back with his initial swing, you may also be hit by the Sword Energy as well.

Cooldown: 10 seconds

Sword Energy Release:

Kai plunges his sword into the ground, releasing explosive sword energy. He will then do it again shortly after over a wider area.

The first attack deals 50%/60% damage and decreases your Chrono Gauge by 50/100.

The second attack deals 70%/80% damage and decreases your Chrono Gauge by 100/200.

Cooldown: 20 seconds

Phase 1: Time Distortion

Dragon Thunder:

Kai rapidly slashes through space to unleash an attack in the form of green pillars. The pillars spawn 5 times appearing from left to right, right to left, and back to left to right, then spreading from the edge to the center and from the center to the edge.

This attack deals 60%/70% damage and decreases your Fever Count by 750/1500. On Hard Mode this attack can slightly knock you back.

To evade this attack, you can stand right on top of a warning area and then hold the down arrow key while pressing Chrono Step, or you can stand next to a warning area and dash right into it as it attacks using Chrono Step.

Holographic Afterimage:

Kai leaves behind an afterimage, moves upwards, and will target you in 3 sequences of 4 attacks with 1 final attack at the end. Be warned though that the final attack is slightly delayed.

Each attack deals 60%/70% damage and decreases your Fever Count by 500/1000.

This is by far the most difficult attack in the first phase. To properly evade it, make sure you have your game audio on and use Chrono Steps to the sound of each attack. The sequence goes:

4 attacks -> very small pause -> 4 attacks -> very small pause -> 4 attacks -> very long pause -> final attack

It can take a bit to get the timing on the final attack down, but with enough practice it becomes easily manageable.

Lasers:

Kai appears from the top corner of the map and fires lasers that sweep across the entire map. The laser rotates from a horizontal direction to a vertical direction relative to the ground or vice versa. The laser will appear 7 times, alternating between the left and right sides.

This attack deals 60%/70% damage and decreases your Fever Count by 1000/2000.

This one is not hard to evade but it is the most punishing if you get hit. Getting hit by this attack only once in Hard Mode can completely ruin your chances at reaching 10,000 Fever Count. For the safest outcome. Stand in the middle of the map between each laser and then Chrono Step towards the laser that is firing at you.

Phase 2

Phase 2 is essentially just a more dangerous version of Phase 1. I will be copy pasting all of the attacks of phase 1 and then tweaking them based on the upgrades that these attacks have received. However, Time Distortion has received completely new patterns.

Swiftness:

Kai will teleport to you if you are a certain distance away from him, and he will strike you down with his sword from above, followed by another swing right after.

The first swing deals 20%/30% damage, stuns you for 1/4 seconds, and decreases your Chrono Gauge by 30/60.

The second swing deals 20%/30% damage, applies super knockback, and decreases your Chrono Gauge by 30/60.

Cooldown: 5 seconds

Double Swing:

Kai swings his sword upward once after 0.84 seconds, then it is followed by a downward strike after 1.44 seconds. Both attacks have identical range. If you are behind him as he is about to use his second swing, he will change to your direction.

The first swing deals 30%/40% damage and decreases your Chrono Gauge by 30/60.

The second swing deals 30%/40% damage, causes a slight knockback, stuns you for 1.5 seconds, and decreases your Chrono Gauge by 30/60.

Cooldown: 10 seconds

Recall Strike:

An enhanced version of Phase 1’s Recall Strike. Kai displays an area of effect in front of him and then dashes in that direction. Afterwards, he will spawn a hologram that will attack both forward and backwards. If you don’t want to use Chrono Step to evade this attack, you can simply stand on top of him for the hologram attack. Both attacks each deal 30%/40% damage, cause a knock back and a 1.5 second stun, and will also decrease your Chrono Gauge by 50/100.

Cooldown: 10 seconds

Energy Swing:

An enhanced version of Phase 1’s Energy Swing. Kai swings his sword around himself, knocking back nearby targets while also firing energy waves to each side. Then every second for the next 2 seconds, more sword energy waves will spawn. Kai’s main body has a hit box for the initial swing cast, but does not have one for the following energy waves. You can stand on top of him without using Chrono Step to avoid the remaining waves.

Kai’s swing deals 20%/30% damage, causes a knock back and a 1.5 second stun, and will also decrease your Chrono Gauge by 50/100.

The Sword Energy deals 50%/60% damage, causes a 5 second slow effect, and decreases your Chrono Gauge by 70/140.

If Kai manages to knock you back with his initial swing, you may also be hit by the Sword Energy as well.

Cooldown: 10 seconds

Sword Energy Release:

An enhanced version of Phase 1’s Sword Energy Release. Kai plunges his sword into the ground, releasing explosive sword energy. He will then do it again shortly after over a wider area. The second attack will also spawn Sword Energy similar to the Energy Swing attack.

The first attack deals 50%/60% damage and decreases your Chrono Gauge by 50/100.

The second attack deals 70%/80% damage and decreases your Chrono Gauge by 100/200.

The Sword Energy deals 50%/60% damage, causes a 5 second slow effect, and decreases your Chrono Gauge by 70/140.

Cooldown: 20 seconds

Phase 2: Time Distortion

High Speed Slash:

126 laser attacks occur for about 7 seconds (11~12 seconds with Chrono Break active) The maximum number of recharges for Chrono Steps during this attack is limited to only 5 to prevent infinitely spamming it as there are many slashes that spawn in. Each slash deals 35%/50% damage and decreases your Fever Count by 375/750.

Initially this attack looks very overwhelming, but the best course of action is just to stand where you are and only make small movements if necessary to dodge any lasers or hold down and use Chrono Step at your location if things are looking a bit excessive.

Great Whirlwind:

Kai will disappear and then leave warning signs on the map. Shortly after these warning signs are displayed, a massive full map attack occurs alongside some blue safe zones that you will want to use Chrono Step around in order to get your recharge refunded to you. You don’t have to use Chrono Step around the safe zone to completely dodge the attack, but just be aware that if you don’t then you won’t get refunded, and you will need at least a couple refunds to fully evade this pattern. Each attack deals 30%/40% damage two times and decreases your Fever Count by 500/1000.

Rotating Lasers:

Kai creates and controls cross shaped lasers on the top of the map, rotating them to attack you. Shortly after, Kai will rise into the sky while rotating the lasers in the opposite direction at a much faster speed. This attack deals 60%/70% damage while also decreasing your Fever Count by 750/1500. 

Similar to the lasers in Phase 1, you’ll want to stand in the middle of the map and use Chrono Step towards the lasers going into your direction. At the end, the faster lasers can feel a little overwhelming, but it will go by very fast.

Death

After clearing Kai, a short death animation occurs, and then you will be transported to the reward room.

Evaluation

Probably the shortest boss guide I have ever written. But it makes sense considering it is an event boss and it is not meant to be a high scale Late/End game boss. Honestly, I love this fight. I initially went into it blind for a min clear on Normal Mode for the very first time and it probably took me around 2-3 hours of practice to learn and clear the boss. The Chrono Step system to evade attacks feels kind of similar to parrying in The First Adversary, so I think however players felt about that boss, they will more than likely feel the same way about Kai.

Normal Mode has very easy access for anyone who gets into the game starting on Challengers World 3 with all of the starter events. Especially on Global MapleStory with the implementation of Rental Equipment. Anyone who actually cares enough about the game that puts in a little bit of effort will be strong enough to challenge Normal Kai in no time.
