MapleStory Complete Malefic Star Guide

By iSIingGunz

This guide will be covering specifics for every difficulty in this fight. There will be attacks and mechanics in this fight that hit or act differently depending on the difficulty, so they will be labeled in a “Normal/Hard” format.

Malefic Star is a boss that is available for all players Level 280 and above who have completed the Arteria prequests. While it’s not exactly in Arteria, this is the official boss within the Arteria region (rip Reina). Normal Mode acts as somewhat of a mid game boss while Hard Mode is definitely considered late game. However, players who can solo Hard Mode are definitely at the end game level. On release, soloing Hard Mode will arguably be the most challenging accomplishment on Heroic Worlds in Global MapleStory.

I want to assume GMS will release this boss with the latest KMS nerf to Normal Mode that happened a month after release. With the nerf, the boss rivals Normal Kaling (post-kaling nerf) in terms of damage required, possibly even lower. Before the nerf, it acted as the bridge between Normal Kaling and Chaos Kalos.

Hard Mode never received an HP nerf like Normal Mode did. The game will tell you that this boss is the bridge between Hard Limbo and Hard Baldrix, but it is way closer to Hard Limbo than anything. It’s even possible that a true min cut run of this boss requires slightly less damage than Hard Limbo.

General Rules

This is the boss entrance cutscene. As mentioned previously, there are no phase transition animations, and phase changes happen within the fight at 80% and 40%.

You will play this fight in both the Illusion Room and the Reality Room. While the Illusion Room is fairly easy to play, the Reality Room presents you a high risk high reward option. In this room, you can either choose to weaken the boss patterns and suffer up to a -10% final damage loss, or strengthen them and in return receive up to +30% final damage.

Malefic Star cannot be slowed down by any attacks. She also has a resistance to Zero’s Critical Bind.

Gauge System

This gauge is shared across the entire party.

The way this gauge works differs depending on if you are playing in the Illusion Room or the Reality Room.

Illusion Room

The Illusion Magic (Outer Yellow) gauge begins completely filled, at a value of 1000. You will have to cleanse on the Essence of Illusion that will occasionally spawn throughout the fight to bring it down by 200 for each cleanse.

When the Essence of Illusion spawns, the designated player only has around 1~1.5 seconds depending on the pattern to cleanse the Essence, or else it will disappear and there will be no effect. In the 3rd Phase of the fight, there are 2 new Essence of Illusion patterns where the boss will spawn 2 Essences with 1 pattern. Both of these Essences must be cleansed to receive the effect.

In the Boss Preparation Room, 1 of the 3 party members must use NPC chat on this statue to be the designated player for both cleansing the Essences, and controlling the Altars that spawn in the Reality Room (more on that later). If the party enters the boss without interacting with this Orb, then the party leader will become the designated player by default.

Control of these things CANNOT be given to another party member throughout the fight unless the designated player dies out, disconnects, or leaves the party.

The inner gauge (displayed completely filled in the image) shows the cooldown of when the Essence of Illusion will spawn. If the gauge is completely filled, that means the Essence can spawn at any moment. The designated player has to be near the boss.

The base cooldown of the Essence of Illusion is 15 seconds, and will increase for each time a party member gets hit by any pattern excluding the ones spawned in by the Essence:

Solo: +4 seconds per hit

Duo: +2 seconds per hit

Trio: +1.33 seconds per hit

The maximum cooldown on the Essence of Illusion Spawning is 35 seconds. If the inner gauge is shown empty, that means the current cooldown is above 20 seconds.

Reality Room

The Illusion Magic gauge displays the difficulty of the fight. The more filled up it is, the more challenging the fight becomes.

Stage 1 (0~249): No effect takes place. The fight is very easy.

Stage 2 (250~499): Getting hit by attacks will increase your Predation

Stage 3 (500~749): The Floating Disaster will appear on the map

Stage 4 (750~1000): Increased damage taken from attacks 

The Inner Gauge displays the Final Damage effect you are currently receiving in this room (Ranging from 90% (-10%) to 130% (+30%))

When you cleanse the Essence of Illusion in the Reality Room, you will be presented with 2 Altars. You have 7 seconds to choose which Altar you want to select, or else the game will decide for you randomly.

The Altar on the left side will decrease the Illusion Magic gauge by 250, and your final damage will be reduced.

The Altar on the right side will increase the Illusion Magic gauge by 250, and your final damage will be increased.

Final Damage adjustments depend on the value of the Illusion Magic AFTER using any of the Altars. The higher the gauge is, the more your final damage will either be increased or decreased.

In the Reality Room, the Essence of Illusion will always have a 15 second cooldown no matter what.

Predation

On top of already having a regular personal death count, you also have Predation. Unlike the regular death count, this number is displayed for the entire party, and the entire party fails and is removed from the fight if this number is to reach 1000.

This number increases when hit by any pattern that generates the Essence of Illusion (In both Illusion and Reality). It also increases when hit by any pattern in the Reality Room if the Illusion Magic gauge is above 250.

This number also passively increases in both rooms if the following conditions are met:

Illusion: If there is less than 60 seconds on the clock for a forced swap, the Predation increases by 2 per second.

Reality: If the Outer Gauge fills up at 1000, a 40 second timer begins before being forcibly swapped. During this time, the Predation increases by 1 per second.

This border will appear around the map whenever any of these conditions are met.

Cleansing the Essence of Illusion in any room will decrease the Predation by 40.

Failure to cleanse the Essence of Illusion will do the following depending on the room you are in:
Illusion Room: Increase the Illusion Magic gauge by 100/200.

Reality Room: Increase the Predation by 50/60.

As I go through the Predation increase values in this guide, they will be based on only 1 person. You can divide accordingly for 2 or 3 man parties.

When fighting in any of the rooms, you will begin with a 3 minute and 40 second timer (3 minutes and 37 seconds on boss entry) before you MUST swap to the opposite room (Of course, only the designated party member can conduct these room swaps).

The designated party member must use the Reality Check/Illusion Projection skill (NPC/Harvest key) to swap from one room to the other. It is important to note that if you use the Reality Check skill (moving to the Reality Room), your final damage in the Reality room will begin at -10% if the Illusion Magic gauge is not completely empty.

TIP FOR DESIGNATED PLAYER: Try to have your NPC/Harvest Key somewhere during the fight where you can’t easily fat finger it. Doing so could make you move to another room sooner than you’ll probably want to and ruin the whole flow of the fight.

Whenever this timer reaches 40 seconds in the Reality room due to the completely filled Illusion Magic gauge, you can cleanse the Essence and use the Altar to bring back the original timer. You also cannot swap from the Reality Room to the Illusion Room if the Illusion Magic is full, so it’s important to cleanse to decrease it.

If you allow the time limit to expire in the reality room due to the full Illusion Magic gauge, a cutscene will play out and you will be forcibly swapped to the opposite room. This forced swap increases the Predation by 160/200.

Essence of Illusion Patterns

When Malefic Star spawns the Essence of Illusion, you will have to deal with certain attack patterns that get in your way of trying to cleanse the Essence. These attacks can occur in both the Illusion and Reality Rooms. These attacks will increase the Illusion Magic gauge by 120 and Predation by 60.

Starlight Echo:

After 1.8 seconds, Malefic Star summons a sphere in front and causes it to explode. Afterwards, a second attack occurs on both sides after 1.56 seconds, followed by a third attack slightly further on both sides after another 1.56 seconds.

The Essence of Illusion spawns at the location of the sphere 1.29 seconds after she begins to cast this pattern. This attack deals 30% damage (50% in Reality Room Stage 4) initially, while the second and third attacks each deal 40% damage (70% in Reality Room Stage 4).

Reinforced Starlight Echo (Phase 2 onward):

The center of this attack will change from the front of Malefic Star to the position of the designated player.

Heavenly Waltz (Phase 2 onward):

Malefic Star summons a sphere in front of her and then dashes forward after 2 seconds. The Essence of Illusion spawns at the location of the sphere and will activate as soon as the first dash occurs. This initial dash deals 30% damage (50% in Reality Room Stage 4).

After the dash, she will move back to the starting location, and will cause spikes to appear in front or behind her. You can identify where these spikes will spawn by looking at the motion of her arms as well as the way the spikes are facing as she is moving back. The spikes deal 40% damage (70% in Reality Room Stage 4).

Forward: Both arms raised up + spikes in front of her

Backwards: One arm raised up, other is down + spikes behind her

Enhanced Heavenly Waltz (Phase 3 onward):

The Essence of Illusion is summoned at the landing point of the initial charge. This attack is just like the regular Heavenly Waltz, but she goes for 1 additional spike charge at the end. The Essence of Illusion is activated right after the 1st spike attack.

Breath of Radiance (Phase 3 onward):

Lasers will spawn twice across the entire map. You must position yourself inside of the lasers to evade the 2nd hit. This is one of the patterns where the Essence of Illusion spawns twice and you need to cleanse them both for the effects to occur. For each laser pattern, the Essence will spawn at a random location on the map. It is the designated player’s job to locate the Essence before the lasers attack, as that is also when the Essence will activate.

Each laser attack deals 60% damage (90% in Reality Room Stage 4).

Sonata of the Nebula (Phase 3 onward, Hard Mode Exclusive):

Stars are fired towards your location a total of 6 times at 0.9 second intervals. The stars will aim at you for 0.3 seconds before being fired in a straight line. Out of the 6 times you are attacked, 2 of these attacks will randomly include the Essence of Illusion. You must cleanse them both for the effects to occur. Each star deals 25% damage (45% in Reality Room Stage 4).

The Illusion Room

This is where the battle begins upon entry. Her entry animation is quite long, however, by the time she lands, you can hit her without having to wait for the animation to finish.

REMEMBER: Getting hit in this room DELAYS the appearance of the Essence of Illusion. Be very careful!

This is a personal pattern. One of the spirits will appear around you and then try to attack you. This attack deals 30% damage and causes a very light knockback without any stagger.

A star shaped warning effect appears on the map. Afterwards, a triangular prism is created and explodes after a short delay. The first prism explodes 1.6 seconds after it is created, and afterwards other prisms are created throughout the map at 0.66 second intervals and explode 0.51 seconds after creation. This attack deals 30% damage.

Cooldown: 18 seconds/13 seconds

This pattern has 3 unique cycles:

  1. From center going towards both sides at the same time
  2. From left to right
  3. From right to left

Starting from the second phase, this pattern is enhanced. Another set of prisms will spawn right after the last with the same cycle. But this time, the first prism will be destroyed after only 0.75 seconds. This attack deals 40% damage.

Cooldown: 25 seconds/15 seconds

Rewind:

6 decorative objects are embedded throughout the map. Of these, 2 (3 from Phase 2 onward) emit lasers in 6 directions after 1.29 seconds. This attack deals 30% damage.

Cooldown: 5 seconds/3.5 seconds

In Phase 3, 4 decorations emit lasers. This attack deals 40% damage.

Cooldown: 7 seconds/3.5 seconds

These lasers are mostly dodged by having to crouch (if they are attacking the bottom), or jumping in the air for a safe spot within the map. When the fight begins, it’s not too tough to dodge them, but they’ll end up getting in your way a ton by the time you reach Phase 3.

Ranunculus (Phase 3):

This is an area that spawns on the left, center, or right side of the map. If you enter the area, a blooming pattern dealing 15% damage will occur 4 times.

Cooldown: 10 seconds

Illusion Room: Malefic Star Patterns

Flash of Illusion:

A magic attack will appear in front of Malefic Star and she will attack you after 1.38 seconds. This attack deals 40% damage.

Cooldown: 5 seconds

When this pattern is cast, Fantasy Prism cannot be used for 3 seconds.

Illusionary Eruption:

Malefic Star will attack both in front and behind her after a cast animation of 1.5 seconds. You can stand on top of her to avoid this attack. This attack deals 45% damage.

 Cooldown: 13 seconds/5 seconds

When this pattern is cast, Fantasy Prism cannot be used for 5 seconds.

The Abyss of Delusion:

Everyone will see the summon effect, but you will get your own individual black hole. When the black hole spawns on top of you, you will become airborne and pulled to the center of the hole. You need to rush or teleport out of the black hole. This attack deals 40% damage if you are still inside of the hole when it disappears.

Cooldown: 40 seconds

This messes with your character movement a little bit, but it will return back to normal after you resurface back on the ground when you exit the hole.

Phantom Rush:

Malefic Star will prepare a dash and then dash forward after 1.44 seconds. You cannot avoid this attack by crouching. This attack deals 20% damage.

Cooldown: 25 seconds/20 seconds

Eye of Dreams (Phase 2 onward):

An Eye will appear at the top center of the map to signal the attack location. Where the eye will attack depends on where most of the party is located. It will either attack the left, center, or right side of the map. Once the signal is made, 8 semi circular attacks occur in that area at 0.36 second intervals. The attack occurs 1.02 seconds after the signal and deals 30% damage.

Cooldown: 30 seconds/25 seconds

When this pattern is cast, Rewind cannot be used for 3 seconds.

Cooldown: 28 seconds/20 seconds

The Reality Room

Before I go into the patterns for this Room, do not forget about the main gimmick of this map. You can weaken the patterns in exchange for losing final damage (up to -10%), or you can strengthen the patterns in exchange for gaining final damage (up to +30%).

Death

And just like that, Malefic Star has been defeated. The boss itself does not have very many patterns, but the way this boss is approached is like no other.

Strategies

For the first time in any of my boss guides, I am going to include a Strategy section. This is only being implemented because there are multiple ways you can fight this boss. Here are the 2 core ways to challenge Malefic Star:

  1. High Risk High Reward (Method for lower spec min clears, very difficult)
  2. The Safe Route (Method for high spec players who do not need the final damage increases in the Reality Room to clear the boss. This method takes much longer to clear but it will make Malefic Star feel like the easiest boss in the game)

There are a couple different ways you can approach the High Risk High Reward Strategy, so let’s get into that first. Note that the 2 High Risk builds listed below have a tag for burst classes and dpm classes. But if you are genuinely trying to follow any of these builds despite your class being labeled as one or the other. I recommend you do both and see what you think is better for you.

Strategy: High Risk High Reward 1

This is a high damage build that favors burst classes.

These are my personal notes taken after watching and reviewing a very low spec Hard Mode solo done by an Ice/Lightning Mage on KMS. Everything down below explains exactly what this person was able to do in order to clear this boss.  

At the beginning of the fight, you will move to the Reality Room after you cleanse the first Essence in the Illusion Room. This is done after the entry Origin Burst, and immediately before the spawning of the 2nd Essence of Illusion. This is done to get the Essence much sooner in that room and you don’t have to face the 10% final damage penalty until after you swap. And when you do, it’s only for a few seconds.

Essences will generally spawn much faster in the Reality Room because you are not at risk of increasing the cooldown on their spawn. Because the Illusion Magic Gauge is already so high, you gain +15% final damage every time you use the right altar. You can use the right altar twice before having to cleanse the left to avoid the forced swap.

You will then use the right altar 2 more times after using the left to bring the final damage up to +30% right in time for your second burst.

So the order of the final damage in the Reality Room as you enter it should look like this:

90% -> 105% -> 120% -> 105% -> 120% -> 130%  

Cleanse the Essence that spawns right after your second burst, and if you are really sweaty, you can wait out the 7 second Altar timer and use the left one at the very end of it.

For the most part, the order in which you are using the Altars in the Reality Room will always be Right -> Right -> Left. It is possible to squeeze out another burst with +30% Final Damage right before you need to hurry up and move back to the Illusion Room.

Once you move back to the Illusion Room, you need to try your best to not get hit so you can quickly cleanse 2 Essence of Illusions. 

Swap back to the Reality Room immediately before the next cleanse. Your gauge should look something like this:

At this time, you have somewhere around 40 seconds before your 2nd Origin burst. Cleanse 3 Essences and use the right altar 3 times to get +30% final damage back for your 2nd Origin. During this burst it is important to NOT REGULAR BIND the boss so you have time to cleanse the essence and use the left altar.

Proceed to use the Right -> Right -> Left order and you should have +30% final damage again for your 5th burst.

A bit over 30 seconds after the initial cast of the 5th burst, you’ll want to return back to the Illusion Room.

Cleanse the Essence twice, and right as you notice the next one will be up soon, swap back to the Reality Room so you can get it there. Cleanse Essences and stall the right Altar accordingly and very carefully so you can have enough time for +30% final damage on your 6th burst.

Once again, you will follow the Altar use order. You should have +30% final damage on your 3rd Origin Burst before leaving the Reality Room this time.

For the rest of the fight, you are pretty much doing the exact same thing:

  • Swap to Illusion Room, Cleanse 2 Essences and swap back to Reality Room right before the next Essence.
  • Cleanse 3 Essences and use the right Altar 3 times before the next burst. Cleanse and use the left Altar after your burst.
  • Do Right -> Right -> Left until after your 2nd burst that round in the Reality Room, in which you swap to the Illusion Room and repeat the process.

I was not joking when I said this was very difficult. Especially when it comes down to stalling that 7 second timer before having to pick an Altar simply so you aren’t forcibly removed from the map.

For anyone attempting this build, I wish you the best of luck because you are going to need it. Even small things like getting hit a bit too many times when trying to gather Essences in the Illusion Room can completely ruin it.

Strategy: High Risk High Reward 2

This is a high risk build that favors dpm classes.

These are my personal notes taken after watching and reviewing a very low spec Hard Mode solo done by a Blaster on KMS. Everything down below explains exactly what this person was able to do in order to clear this boss.  

At the beginning of the fight, you will stay put in the Illusion Room until the Illusion Magic Gauge is completely depleted. 

You will burst in the Illusion Room twice and if you cleanse properly without taking too much damage you should be in the Reality Room with slightly over a minute remaining in the Illusion Room.

You will swap to the Reality Room without any final damage reduction.

 Cleanse the next 3 essences and take the Right Altar to receive +30% final damage without being on a 40 second countdown and being forced to take the Left Altar.

You can hold +30% final damage for the remaining time of the Reality Room. However, you will not cleanse any more essences during this time. This is because cleansing an essence means an Altar, whether it’s left or right, must be triggered.

Getting hit by any patterns during this time can make your Predation value go up very very high.

Fortunately, your time in the Illusion Room cleansing the Essences once again can bring the Predation down by 200 (40 each for 5 cleanses).

You once again immediately bring the gauge back up to +30% final damage and keep it there before you have to swap right after your 3rd Origin burst.

And this is pretty much all you have to do for the 2nd build:

  • Start in Illusion Room and bring the Illusion Magic Gauge down to 0 before swapping to the Reality Room
  • Increase your final damage to +30% without reaching 1000 on the Illusion Magic Gauge. Try your best to avoid getting hit so you can keep the gauge below 1000 and keep your Predation low. Swap back to the Illusion Room as the timer approaches 0.
  • Repeat the Process.

This strategy sounds simpler, but can be way more challenging due to the fact that not cleansing any essences means your Predation value does not go down as often as it should.

Strategy: Normal/Safe Build

This is for the non sweaty players. But because it’s so simple, I won’t really talk about it for too long.

You enter the fight and you follow the entire first part of the 2nd High Risk build. You should be able to do so up until the point where you get that +30% final damage without filling up the Illusion Magic Gauge.

However, this is where things can get a bit too challenging. If you ever feel like the Predation goes up a little too much for your liking, you will cleanse the essences and take the left Altar to weaken the patterns. Continue to cleanse left until one of the following:

  1. Timer runs out and you need to go back to the Illusion Room
  2. You and your party feel comfortable enough again to strengthen the patterns for more final damage.

When you return back to the Illusion Room, the Illusion Magic Gauge will return back to 1000. You must stay in this room while this gauge is high if you want to avoid increasing your Predation as much as possible.

In order to play completely safe to avoid a fail out due to reaching 1000 Predation, you must:

  1. Stay in the Illusion Room until you have completely erased the yellow gauge or if there is only 1 minute left in the room (REMINDER that your Predation goes up by 2 every second there is less than a minute remaining).
  2. Play the Reality Room on Stage 1 and continue to take the left altar. Your final damage will be reduced by 10% but the only thing giving you Predation is if you get hit by any of the Essence of Illusion Patterns.

I will ALWAYS ALWAYS ALWAYS recommend your party gives strengthening the patterns to increase your final damage a shot if you guys are up for it. But if you are getting messed up and on the verge of failing, this is the way to handle that situation. This super safe build is only really available for players whose damage is so high that they can afford to play like this.

Evaluation

This is the first boss of its kind to be released, allowing it to be so incredibly easy or so incredibly hard depending on how much damage you want to deal in the fight. It’s a boss that has easy access to everyone due to how soft it is when you play safe.

 It’s crazy that the slider can go from incredibly easy to incredibly difficult. There is so much to keep in mind during those High Risk builds (especially the 1st one) that it would make perfect sense for someone attempting a Hard Mode solo to have a coach helping them to make sure they remember everything they’re supposed to do. There is a lot to keep track of so it’s easy to forget about some small things.

I know there have been some people with complaints about newer bosses recently (since Limbo came out), but I think this one is pretty unique. I didn’t really like it that much at first because I had a lot of trouble understanding it now. But now that I know what I know it’s honestly very well made.
