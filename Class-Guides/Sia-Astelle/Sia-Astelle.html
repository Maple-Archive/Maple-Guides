Introduction

Sia Astelle arrives in Global MapleStory as a damage-focused magician who channels the light of the stars into a fluid combat style. Her design emphasizes consistency and rhythm: weaving skills together to create Constellation Skills, creating a natural flow between wide-reaching AoEs for clearing mobs and high damage burst skills for bosses. Her 5th Job skill “Shine” enables the use of Constellation Skills without combo limitations for a short period of time, allowing a quick succession of strong summon skills for a powerful burst. This gives her a flexible toolkit that adapts well to MapleStory’s current burst-centric meta.

Sia Astelle has a unique 6th job mechanic called “Erda Link” in place of other classes’ HEXA Matrix. Currently, Sia Astelle has two Mastery Skills, “SHINE Ray/SHINE Antares” and “SHINE Boom/SHINE Algol/SHINE Fomalhaut”, that adds a massive damage boost to Sia’s kit. Erda Link is very rewarding early-game and mid-game, requiring little investment for high % Final Damage gain. She is the perfect class for new players, returning players, as a Legion Champion/Challenger World/bossing mule, and those who enjoy other teleport mage classes.


WHAT MAKES SIA ASTELLE SHINE?

  • Strong and Simple Bossing
  • Easy burst rotation that consists of pressing a list of buffs in order (most of which are in the Skill Sequence), placing down all of our summons, then spamming our filler skill (Ray).
  • Our skills read like we are a combo class, but our 5th job skill “Shine” completely obsoletes that mechanic.
  • Currently, we are the strongest low hexa (<8k fragments) class in the game. This means until you reach max 6th job farming you will be much stronger than people with similar gear and fragments.
  • Good Defensive Coverage
  • Sadalmelik (aka Chalice) is a 2-second (3-second if casting with Stellagram Fusion) invincibility frame (iFrame) skill with a 90-second cooldown that also gives us a 12-second lasting damage reduction after use (including %HP skills).
  • Ethereal Form is a common mage node in our 5th job on a 60s CD that gives a 3s iframe.
  • In late game, upon finishing your Genesis liberation you will gain access to the common genesis iframe skill. With these three skills, we have plenty of defensive options to help us stay alive throughout a fight.
  • Tied for Longest Bind
  • Our class bind “Time Binder” is a 13 second bind lasting 3 seconds longer than normal binds.
  • As you progress into harder difficulty bosses - the ability to keep a boss bound longer allows a larger window to confirm your damage. This proves to be a very underrated support skill in bosses such as Seren, Extreme Lotus, and Kalos to name a few.
  • Subjectively Good Mobility
  • Love it or hate it - Sia is a teleport class. This means we have very snappy and precise movement which proves invaluable in bossing for accurate dodges. Teleport also means access to telecasting - the ability to cast/attack while teleporting.
  • We have a spammable up jump + float similar to explorer magicians. While we also have access to the 5th job skill “Blink” that every other class must use to float in the air for boss mechanics - ours has no cooldown and is a very strong option in certain bosses like Lotus and Kalos to dodge OHKO attacks
  • Sia is very cute

POTENTIAL TURNOFFS

  • Very Button / Keybind / Skill Sequence Heavy
  • With so many buttons, our key bindings can become quite filled and cluttered. Our 2-min burst Skill Sequence is so long that every available slot is filled, and it will hold your character in place for 1-2 seconds when casting.
  • Teleport Class
  • While I listed it as a pro above, to some people this is a major con. TP is slower in raw map coverage so when farming or wanting fast movement TP is slower than our flash jump counterparts.
  • Global MS exclusive / Non Korean MS class
  • If you are active in the maple community sphere, this is a common pain point for classes like Kanna or Hayato who have very infrequent dev updates leading to bug fixes and balancing being very sporadic or not addressed.
  • However! Sia was riddled with bugs on release and they have all been addressed/fixed in a very timely manner. We are confirmed to be developed by the KMS dev team unlike our other non KMS brethren, so we have hope! (and lots of copium!!)

How to Do Damage

Sia has three distinct phases when it comes to dealing damage, which includes her:

(i) Main Burst (every 2 mins, potentially with origin);

(ii) Mini Burst (1 min after main burst); and

(iii) Off-Burst DPM between main and mini bursts.

Before breaking down what you should be doing in these phases, let's discuss Sia's setup prior to entering a boss fight.

Locked vs Unlocked Skills 🔒

Sia's constellation skills can be "locked" by right clicking the skill icon in her skills tab. This prevents constellation skills from being cast even if it is off cooldown and Sia completes the markings required to activate a constellation skill.

Below is an example of what a skill icon looks like when “locked”:

There are a few skills that you may want to consider locking.

Antares

Casting ray will naturally complete the constellation for Antares making it cast so long as you have stacks of Antares available to use. Depleting stacks of Antares before bursting can be undesirable as it's better to use up stacks when your burst buffs are up. Also a boss may move away from where your rabbit holes are.

Alchiba, Bellatrix, Capella, Canopus, Sirius:

These skills have a cooldown that is perfectly aligned with Sia's Shine buff (and all of these should be used with Sia's skill sequences) so there is virtually no opportunity for these to be cast outside of Shine. It's best to have these locked in case you accidentally complete their constellation outside of your main/mini burst.

(Optional) Sadalsuud:

To get the full constellation FD bonus on Sadalsuud, it should either be:

a) Unlocked and cast via Shine (after emptying/resetting Sia's stellagram); or

b) Locked (or unlocked) and cast via the Sadalsuud skill itself after Shine is cast.

Although either of the above methods can be used, locking has the additional benefit of allowing back-to-back mini bursts to be used without accidentally using Sadalsuud. This is because our mini burst requires casting Shine. If your Stellagram is empty prior to casting Shine and Sadalsuud is available, it will cast Sadalsuud even if you only intended to use a mini burst (this will be explained later).

(Optional) Sadalmelik:

If you use Link as a self heal (whilst having an empty stellagram), then you may accidentally cast Sadalmelik, which is Sia's class iframe. It can be a good idea to lock Sadalmelik if you intend to use Link every now and again. If you don't use Link ever, then you may keep this unlocked to have an extra long iframe.

(Very Optional) Vega:

Vega may be accidentally cast if you use the marking skill Link :Link: as a self heal since its constellation is complete with any combination of three rays and a link cast. This can potentially be undesirable as you may want to save the skill to fit inside Sia's main or mini burst buffs.

Other constellations?

Fomalhaut - Unlikely to be cast accidentally given that you need several Booms to complete its constellation. It is also a good idea to have this unlocked so that you can choose to manually combo this skill during Sia's off-burst DPM phase (see subsequent sections).

Algol - Also unlikely to be cast accidentally given that you need to cast Boom. It's also best to keep this unlocked so that you can manually cast Algol before entering a boss fight.

Skill Sequence

When setting up your Skill Sequence for your 2-minute burst, there are minor differences based on whether you’re running Sadalsuud locked or unlocked. There are two viable approaches, and they both perform the same overall:

  • If Sadalsuud is unlocked, you should include both Stellar Reset and Shine at the beginning of your Sequence (since Shine alone on the Stellagram will summon Sadalsuud).
  • If Sadalsuud is locked, you can skip Stellar Reset and include Sadalsuud directly in your Sequence instead.

Both setups use the same number of slots and perform the same overall.

2-minute Burst Skill Sequences:

Sadalsuud Locked 🔒

  • In order: Shine, Observe, Maple World Goddess’s Blessing, Tree of Stars, Sadalsuud, Alchiba, Bellatrix, Savior’s Circle, Canopus, Sirius, Fomalhaut, Capella, Appear, Terms & Conditions, RoR.

Sadalsuud Unlocked 🔑

  • In order: Stellar Reset, Shine, Observe, Maple World Goddess’s Blessing, Tree of Stars, Alchiba, Bellatrix, Savior’s Circle, Canopus, Sirius, Fomalhaut, Capella, Appear, Terms & Conditions, RoR.

Mini-burst Skill Sequence:

  • In order: Shine, Celestial Alignment (optional), Alchiba, Bellatrix, Canopus, Sirius, Fomalhaut, Capella, Terms & Conditions, Weapon Jump Int.
  • Why is Celestial Alignment here? There is currently a bug that does not apply Shine’s benefits when Shine-dependent skills are placed directly after it in a Skill Sequence. Celestial Alignment is recommended to add between Shine and its dependent skills as a buffer to prevent this from happening (This is not an issue in the 2-min burst Skill Sequence because enough non-Shine-dependent skills are already after Shine).

Boss Room Preparation

  • Make sure you have the right Special Nodes equipped (e.g. Fatal Strike) and you're on the right link/legion/equip/fam presets.
  • Make sure Starlight and Mana Overload toggles are on.
  • Make sure you have all your boss buffs cast including decent skills and Celestial Alignment (or just rely on pet auto buffs).
  • Summon Algol (Boom x4)
  • Algol will be cast during every 2min burst and lasts for 2 min so this is the only time you'll need to manually combo Algol!

2 min/Origin Burst

(Assumes a RoR/WJ Setup)

  • Note that which ring you use for main or mini-burst depends on your character stats. Typically RoR is used for the main burst unless: (i) Your WJ ring level is higher or (ii) You're a late/end game Sia in regular server (Please note this will be changed to a standard RoR/Cont. setup after the KMS Oz ring changes are implemented in GMS).

Here is a typical order for Sia's main burst:

  1. Use the 2-min Skill Sequence (see 'Skill Sequence' section above)
  2. Bind the boss (use Sia's class bind for the extra duration!) Bind can also be cast before Skill Sequence when dealing with a boss that dashes or moves frequently (use your own discretion here).
  3. Press Vega, Izar, and unload all stacks of Antares, summon Pole and commence spamming Ray.
  4. Recast Sadalsuud once it is fully charged. You can also time the cast with Fatal Strike/special nodes to optimize damage.
  1. How can I tell if Sadalsuud is fully charged? You can tell it has reached full charge if you see a blue ring with white hourglass-shaped sigils spinning around your character and a small hourglass hovering above that:
  1. If Origin is up, press it when either the bind ends or when RoR/WJ has ~6 seconds left whichever comes first (depends on your ring level).
  2. Continue spamming Ray and try to refresh Pole every 10s (or refresh immediately if the boss dashes away!).
  3. If you've locked Antares, remember to unload stacks as they come up (or when the boss dashes), you should be able to fit in 2 additional Antares stacks depending on your Shine duration.
  4. Before Shine ends, refresh Algol (this will last until the next 2 min burst)
  5. If Vega/Fomal/Izar come up before Shine ends, cast these ASAP. Whether or not you get an extra cast inside Sia's Shine buff will depend on your Shine 6th job boost level, cooldown reduction effects, and procs of Distorted Time. Getting lvl 9+ Shine in Erda Link will help fit a second Vega/Fomal, and getting lvl 29 6th job Shine will help fit a second Izar.*

Sia's burst concludes when Shine buff runs out. Hold onto your Antares stacks 🚫 until your mini burst.

Notes:

Our Sequence is rather long and will hold you in place for 1-2 seconds. If the boss is likely to knock you back or dash during this time, feel free to bind first.

If you are facing a boss that is unbindable (e.g. black mage) or if the environmental effects are more dangerous than the boss itself (e.g. Seren), you might want to consider using gene iframe instead of (or with) the initial bind.

Mini Burst

The mini burst phase starts approx. 60s after your main burst (when your 60s cooldown buffs and summons come back up).

Here is a typical mini burst order:

  1. Use Mini burst Skill Sequence (see 'Skill Sequence' section above)
  2. Cast Vega and Izar, unload all stacks of Antares, summon Pole.
  3. Spam Ray whilst remembering to recast Pole every ~10s (or when the boss dashes).
  4. Cast Antares two additional times (depending on Shine level) when stacks refresh.
  5. If Vega/Fomal/Izar come up before Shine ends, cast these ASAP*
  6. Sia's mini burst concludes when Shine buff runs out. Hold onto your Antares stacks 🚫until your main burst.

Off-Burst DPM

This phase begins once your main or mini burst ends. This phase is relatively simple:

  • Spam Ray
  • Refresh Pole every 10s (or every time the boss dashes)
  • If Vega/Fomal/Izar come up AND neither your main nor mini burst is up within 10s, consider casting these otherwise hold until main/mini burst*
  • (Optional) Due to Ascent skills (Starlit Cosmos) not being affected by temporary buffs, it is optimal (damage-wise) to use Ascent during off burst. So feel free to insert some casts here or use it as an iframe up to you!

Notes:

*Since Shine buff is not active, it will be up to you whether to manually combo Vega/Fomal/Izar noting that Fomal gives the largest damage increase (>5x) by comboing. Vega combo is also relatively easy.

MOBBING

Sia Astelle’s main mobbing skill is Boom. Unfortunately, Sia no longer has skills that proc Sol Janus: Dawn for a long period of time after the v. 267 update removed Algol and Bellatrix’s ability to proc it. However, a less efficient but low-effort Sol Janus: Dusk training method is still available. One of our Sia Astelle Discord community members, Lexi, has shared a “lazy” Sol Janus: Dawn training method using Algol and Erda Shower that is less efficient than constantly spamming Boom, but takes considerably less effort.

Sia’s mobbing is heavily centered around map familiarity, spamming Boom, summon management, and efficient Stellagram routing. Compared to many modern mage classes, her base mobbing can initially feel awkward due to limited vertical coverage on Boom, but this is greatly relieved with the introduction of M2: Shine Boom after reaching 6th Job.

Overall, Sia’s mobbing is best described as slightly high-maintenance: continuously spamming Boom to proc Sol Janus. Her farming performance improves significantly once players either level up Sol Janus to level 30, or become comfortable cycling constellations and maintaining summon uptime. Skills such as Canopus and Sirius contribute a large portion of her map coverage, allowing Sia to control multiple sections of a map simultaneously while spamming Boom.

Sia generally performs best on flatter or more compact maps where her horizontal coverage and summon placements can consistently overlap spawn locations. Larger vertical maps can feel less comfortable due to the relatively limited vertical reach of Boom and some Constellation skills outside of Appear-enhanced states. Because of this, Sia tends to favor maps with predictable spawn flow and manageable platform spacing. If you’re looking for specific training maps for your level, we recommend referencing the #map-training channel in the Sia Astelle Discord.

One of Sia’s strengths is that her mobbing scales well with investment and player optimization. Summon uptime management, Erda Fountain placement, and efficient fusion routing all contribute heavily to clear speed. While her efficient farming is more active than many “lazy” farming classes, experienced Sia players can still maintain strong kill consistency once rotations are refined.

Why no CD Skip?

The only true benefit in a rotation is CD Skipping Izar. However Izar only amounts to about 5% of our BA thus 10% boss and 38% buff duration both out value CD skip. Everything else in our kit will cause a heavy desync to our cooldowns.

How do I roll the perfect IA?

*This advice is very dependent on where you are in the game (between beginner to established account) but there are 2 directions you can take. It is HIGHLY recommended to only roll your inner ability during 50% honor off Sunny Sunday events that occur usually once or twice per major event.

Option 1: Pragmatic Approach (Beginners/Early Game should only use honor)

1. Roll with either Honor or Miracle Circulators until you get either Unique Buff Duration (33-38%) or Boss Damage (5-10%) in 2nd or 3rd line.
2. Lock that line then roll for Attack Speed +1 in line 1 or the opposite Unique IA you did not get in line 2/3.
3. Lock that line then roll for the last IA you’re missing ending up with Attack Speed +1, Buff Duration (33-38%), and Boss Damage (5-10%)

Example: Rolled Buff Duration 35% in line 2 > We lock it > Roll Boss Damage 7% > We lock it > Roll Attack Speed +1

**It is important to learn when to settle!! If you have hit Buff Duration 33-38, Attack Speed +1, and are low on honor you can settle on epic/unique abnormal damage or flat magic attack. If you hit AS+1, boss damage 10%, you can settle an epic buff duration line etc.

Option 2: Frugal Approach (I want to use the LEAST resources possible at the cost of potentially bricking)

1. Roll with either Honor or Miracle Circulators until you get Unique Buff Duration (33-38%) in 2nd or 3rd line.
2. Lock that line then roll for Boss Damage (5-10%) in 2nd or 3rd line.
3. Lock that line then roll for Attack Speed +1

Unique Buff Duration is the most expensive IA (tied with Crit Chance) so we are targeting that first. Locking increases the cost of subsequent rolls so we are aiming for the rarer line Boss Damage. Finishing up with Attack Speed +1.

The reason I do not recommend this rolling strategy is because the order of importance is Attack Speed +1 > Buff Duration 33-38 > Boss Damage 5-10. Following this method you could end up with no attack speed +1. The other scenario you can settle on an epic 3rd line while still gaining the bulk of the FD from IA.

Hyper Stats


Every class shares the same hyper stats philosophy and it is often very character specific pending the stats of the individual. You will want to fill out the big 4 (Boss Damage > Crit Damage=Damage > IED) followed by Magic ATT and any remainder 1-5 points into Int/Luk. The amount of points is dependent on your level so you’ll want to follow a rough ratio as you progress or use a calculator.

For a late game setup - we will want to aim for Boss Damage 15, IED 10, Magic ATT 7, 12-13 Damage, 12-13 Crit Damage with Magic ATT/Int/Luk taking any leftover points.

We highly recommend using a hyper stat calculator to optimize. Cap your IED at 10 - the calculator will over value this stat and doesn’t take into account any skills or buffs that increase IED. (Sia has Alchiba that grants 30% IED. Classes like Bishop, Night Lord, and Mercedes to name a few give 40%+IED to the party)

EQUIP WINDOW

Weapon & Secondary

Sia Astelle’s weapon of choice is a Celestial Light. This is a unique weapon specific to Sia Astelle characters. Her secondary weapon is a Compass. Sia is unable to equip shields.

WSE

Sia, like most mage classes, benefits from a 9/0/0 setup of %Magic Attack/%Boss Damage/%IED for her Weapon, Secondary, and Emblem potential lines. For Interactive World players, 9/0/0 is ideal for Bonus Potential (bpot) as well, but players in the midgame range should aim for 3 lines of %Magic Attack on the main potential and 2 lines of %Magic Attack on the bpot, as the amount of cubes it takes on average for a 3-line %Magic Attack bpot is exponentially more costly than 2 lines, and there are many other more lucrative upgrades you can spend your resources on at the midgame range. Loadouts like 8/1/0 and 7/2/0 are also okay when you tread into double prime territory (i.e. if you have 13matk/13matk/30boss on your weapon it is likely going to be better than 13/10/10). We recommend using a sim tool like MapleScouter to figure out what would be best for your character in these scenarios.

If you are still on Gold Guardian Emblem instead of Mitra’s Rage - it is okay to settle on a 12matk/9matk + 1. The last one can be IED > %dmg > %stat in that order of preference.

Our BIS secondary is Princess No’s Compass which drops from Princess No in the form of Captivating Fragment. You’ll need 15 of these fragments and often will take at minimum 2 weeks to get (they are affected by drop rate so make sure you’re in drop gear + legion drop!)

What about CD Hat?

Currently Sia is a stat hat class. Any CD reduction is simply QoL for not misaligning our 30sec and 1min CDs but does not actually increase our output.


We don't have many cooldown-based skills that are repeatedly cycled outside of the burst window like the classes that favor CD hat do. It will only really help you if you're min clearing a boss and need to squeeze in an additional Origin burst into a 30 minute run, or if you're doing Culvert and you're trying to fit in a 2nd burst, but this will have less validity once KMS’s 20-minute boss timer changes come to GMS. Classes do not typically choose to run a CD hat over a stat hat solely for the sake of reducing burst cooldown otherwise.

If you're at a point in the game where you have spare pieces and mesos to spend, then by all means, make a 2nd Eternal hat with CD reduction to use for those scenarios, but your main hat of use should be a stat hat.


However there are some theorycrafts that once our Hexa Shine is maxed or the KMS RoR/Cont ring changes happen that there might be some validity. Until we have a proper test for that tech, it is advised to only use stat hat.

We are however a CD hat abuser in culvert, so you’ll want a -3/-4 one if you’re a culvert sweat.

Oz Ring?


Presently we recommend ring swapping between Ring of Restraint 4 (or lv. 5 or 6, luck permitting) and Weapon Jump Int 4 if you want the most damage. We are a 1min class with a strong 2min burst.

If you don’t have a Weapon Jump Int 4 or can’t be bothered to ring swap, both Ring of Restraint 4 and Continuous Ring 4 by itself are viable options. Please be aware that if you choose to use Continuous Ring 4, it is more optimal to have Antares unlocked, but we recommend RoR over Continuous Ring in nearly all scenarios.

5TH JOB SKILLS

!! This section is currently being updated with new information from the v. 269 patch! There are no more trinodes.

General Tips

  • We recommend putting 1 V Point into Decent Speed Infusion to unlock it immediately since Sia needs the extra attack speed, and also 1 V Point into Decent Combat Orders since it provides around a ~5% FD gain upon unlock.
  • Resetting your V-Matrix points only costs 10m mesos, so if you’re very low on Nodes, you can always reset your V-Matrix to alternate between a bossing or mobbing setup.

Job Nodes

  • If you’re a fresh Sia, start by putting 1 V Point in each Job Node.
  • Prioritize maxing Shine first. This reduces the cooldown to 60 seconds, allowing you to use it in both your burst and mini-burst.
  • Sadalsuud is the next best Job Node you want to max, but if you are Hyper Burning to 260 and are severely Node-limited, balance out your Sadalsuud levels with the prioritized Boost Nodes in the section below so they can be high enough level to unlock M1 and M2.
  • Lastly, level Sirus and Savior’s Circle evenly.
  • If you are Node-limited, prioritize the relevant Boost Nodes below that you need to unlock M1 and M2 over maxing these if you are rushing to 6th Job, since Job Nodes are more expensive to level than Boost Nodes. Don’t get stuck trying to unlock M1 without the required Ray/Antares Boost Node at level 40!

Boost nodes

  • Prioritize maxing Ray/Antares, Boom/Algol, and Pole/Fomalhaut before the other Boost Nodes. Your Ray/Antares Boost Node must be at least level 40 to unlock M1: SHINE Ray/SHINE Antares when you reach 6th job. Boom, Algol, and Fomalhaut need to be at least level 40 to unlock M2: SHINE Boom/SHINE Algol/SHINE Fomalhaut.
  • Pole/Fomalhaut can be prioritized before Boom/Algol if you are focused solely on bossing.
  • Afterwards, the Izar/Vega and Canopus Boost Nodes can be assigned V Points evenly.
  • Max Starlight Boost last.

Common Nodes

  • The Common Nodes are lesser priority than the Job Nodes and the Skill Nodes, but we recommend at least putting 1 V Point in Decent Sharp Eyes, Decent Speed Infusion, Decent Combat Orders, Decent Advanced Blessing, Maple World Goddess’s Blessing, Tree of Stars, and Mana Overload to start.
  • Optional: If you will be training a lot, feel free to max Decent Holy Symbol immediately. You can also opt to put points into Erda Shower to help with training.
  • Optional: For bossing, you can assign a point into Ethereal Form and Decent Holy Fountain for survivability, but we do not recommend maxing these immediately if you are Node-limited. Will of Erda may be helpful if you are having trouble with curses in Black Mage or if you are getting blinded often in Seren. You can also choose to assign a point into Blink and Rope Lift to assist your movement, but these are less important since we have a native float (Starry Wave) and a decent up jump.
  • The maxing order should be: Maple World Goddess’s Blessing > Tree of Stars > Mana Overload > True Arachnid Reflection/Solar Crest (the Nodestones that drop from Will & Seren) > Blink (for the Magic Attack) > Rope Lift (for the stats) > (Decent Sharp Eyes/Speed Infusion/Combat Orders/Advanced Blessing can be assigned V Points evenly). Everything else not listed can be leveled at your own discretion, but some good skills we recommend leveling are Ethereal Form to lower its cooldown, Decent Holy Fountain to increase its healing and reduce its cooldown, and Erda Nova as a backup bind.

Special Nodes

Sia Astelle’s best Special Node is Fatal Strike I. If Ride or Die is out, use the 40% Boss Damage Special Node (Inspector Power I) instead.

6TH JOB SKILLS

Erda Link

Sia Astelle has a unique 6th job system called “Erda Link.” She is currently the only class with the Erda Link system. Compared to Hexa Matrix, Erda Link is more rewarding in the early game, since our early upgrades grant a large amount of final damage without a high Erda Fragment investment. However, Erda Link has a low ceiling– Sia Astelle currently has only two Mastery skills (SHINE Ray/SHINE Antares & SHINE Boom/SHINE Algol/SHINE Fomalhaut). We are awaiting information on when her additional Masteries will be added, and will update the guide as Sia’s kit continues to grow.

How Erda Link works:

When you first unlock Erda Link, you obtain your Origin skill, Celestial Design. The Erda Link board is a tree with 4 branches. Each branch is made up of different “Stones.” To unlock Stones, you must work from the middle of the board outwards. You cannot unlock a Stone without unlocking the one that came before it first.

There are 5 different types of Stones:

Origin Stone 

  • After completing the 6th Job Advancement, the Origin Stone is automatically activated.
  • Can be enhanced up to Level 30.
  • Unlock additional effects at Levels 10, 20, and 30.

Ultimate Stone

  • Enhances 4th Job and lower skills. Equivalent to other class’ Mastery nodes.
  • There are currently only two Ultimate stone: SHINE Ray/SHINE Antares, and SHINE Boom/SHINE Algol/SHINE Fomalhaut.
  • Can be enhanced up to Level 30.
  • Requires the boosted skills in the V Matrix to be Lv. 40 to unlock the Ultimate Stone. (Enhanced slots do not count toward this requirement.)
  • Requires Light Power to be activated.

Skill Stone

  • Enhances 5th Job skills.
  • Activate Common Skills through Skill Stones, such as Sol Janus.
  • Can be enhanced up to Level 30 through one of the following methods:
  • Enhance two separate Skill Stones to 15 levels: Savior’s Circle, Sadalsuud, Shine.
  • Enhance 1 Skill Stone directly to Level 30: Sirius
  • Requires the 5th Job Skill Node to be Lv. 25 to unlock the Skill Stone. Enhanced slots do not count toward this requirement.
  • Requires all connected Rush Stones to be activated before it becomes available.

Boost Stone

  • Grants bonus effects when specific activation conditions are met.
  • Cannot be enhanced.
  • Require Light Power to be activated.

Rush Stone

  • Provides bonus stats.
  • Cannot be enhanced.
  • Becomes available when the previous connected stone is activated.
  • Stones that do not meet activation or enhancement requirements will have the Activate Stone or Enhance Stone button disabled, with a tooltip explaining the unlock conditions when hovered over.
  • Stones that meet the requirements will glow and the corresponding button will be enabled.
  • Activating a stone unlocks new paths, and connected branches will light up.

SHINE Stone

  • SHINE Stones are special stones that provide additional stats and can be enhanced up to Level 20. This is the Erda Link equivalent to Hexa Stat.
  • Starting at Level 11, enhancements may fail and can cause the stone’s level to decrease (think Star Forcing).
  • SHINE Stones are activated and enhanced using Sol Erda, Sol Erda Fragments, and mesos.
  • Each SHINE Stone has specific activation requirements that must be met before it can be activated. There will be six SHINE Stones total, but only 4 will be activatable upon Sia Astelle's release.

Light Power

To unlock Boost Stones and Ultimate Stones, you must utilize Light Power. Light Power is a mechanic specific to Erda Link that lights up a square area around the furthest Stones you have unlocked. To expand your Light Power, you must progress the four branches of your Erda Link tree. Boost Stones and Ultimate Stones within the zone of your Light Power become unlockable.

The Light Power unlocking mechanic only applies to the free-standing Boost Stones and Ultimate Stones. Stones that are connected to the tree do not need to be in your Light Power zone to be unlocked.

Mastery I: SHINE Ray/SHINE Antares

To unlock SHINE Ray/SHINE Antares, your Ray/Antares V-Skill node must be at least level 40, and the SHINE Ray/SHINE Antares Ultimate Stone must be in the lit up zone of your Light Power. You can check this by toggling the Light Power view in the bottom-right of the Erda Link UI. You can expand your Light Power zone by unlocking either Sol Janus or the "2% Normal Enemy Damage" Rush Stone immediately to the left of Sol Janus, AND the "+200 LUK" Rush Stone (down).

  • Recommended for Sia mains (or for those who are planning on grinding a lot): Unlock Sol Janus.
  • Recommended for Sia mules: Unlock the 2% Normal Enemy Damage Rush Stone on the left branch instead (you do not need Janus unlocked despite its placement).

Unlocking M1 via Sol Janus costs 10 Sol Erda Energy and 225 Fragments total, compared to just 4 Sol Erda Energy and 150 Fragments through the 2% Normal Enemy Damage route. As a result, choosing Janus is only worth taking if you were already planning to unlock it for training.

Mastery II: SHINE Boom/SHINE Algol/Shine Fomalhaut

The M2 Ultimate Stone is located on the bottom right. Like M1, the M2 stone must fall within your Light Power’s active area to be unlocked. You can check this by toggling the Light Power view in the bottom-right of the Erda Link UI. If the M2 stone isn’t within your lit-up area, you’ll need to expand your Light Power by unlocking a few prerequisite Rush Stones:

  • To expand Light Power downwards: - Unlock the +200 LUK Rush Stone on the left of M2 (this should already be unlocked if you have M1 already).
  • To expand Light Power towards the right: Unlock the Ignore Enemy DEF +2% Rush Stone at the beginning of the right branch, and any one of the three Ignore Enemy DEF +1% Rush Stones above M2.

Other Stones

Is Fruits of Mastery busted?

  • No. Prioritize Janus level 30 over unlocking this. From testing it is about a ~33-35sec (with server lag) uptime with ~150sec downtime - comes out to only 7% increased exp.

Is Fragment of Distorted Time busted?

  • We need further testing, but from our initial impression, it’s not something to prioritize. It can occasionally grant an additional Izar proc during Shine, and reduce the cooldown of Pulse (Hero’s Will). Other than that, there are no other notable advantages.
